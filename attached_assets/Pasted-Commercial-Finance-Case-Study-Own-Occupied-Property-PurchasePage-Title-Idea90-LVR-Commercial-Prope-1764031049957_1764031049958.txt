The Client's Goal & Challenge (The Problem)SectionDetailKey TakeawayClient StatusA business owner operating out of a rented factory.Currently paying $129,000 per annum in rent.GoalTo purchase a new, own-occupied commercial property to secure premises and build equity.Move from a renter to an owner/landlord of his own business premises.The ChallengeLimited Cash Deposit due to critical working capital requirements within the existing business.Needed a high Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR), which is difficult for commercial property.2. Our Strategic Solution (The Strategy)Market Review: Conducted an extensive review of the commercial lending market.The Key Win: We were able to source a specialist commercial lender willing to approve a 90% Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR) for the property purchase.Rate Structure: The interest rate was within the same range as standard commercial lending, with only a small, manageable premium paid.Structure:Establishment of a New SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) / Company: This new entity was set up specifically to purchase and hold the commercial property.Leaseback Arrangement: The client's existing business was then able to lease the property back from the new SPV and pay rent to its own company.3. The Positive Outcome (The Results)A strategic win that delivered long-term stability and cash flow control.Property Acquisition: The client successfully purchased his new own-occupied commercial property.Cash Flow Retention: The high-LVR finance strategy allowed the client to retain the vital cash flow (working capital) needed for daily business operations and growth.Future Foundation: The client is now building equity in an asset, has stabilized his premises costs, and has a foundation for his next stage of growth.Ongoing Partnership: We continue to assist the client by mapping out and executing short-to-medium-term equipment finance needs through an established plan.4.
